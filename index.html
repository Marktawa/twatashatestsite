Twatasha Disabled and OVC's Organisation

Welcomes You!

Twatasha Disabled and OVC Organization (TDOVC) is a Non- Governmental Organization, non-partisan and non-profit making community-based organisation that is located in Kafue District 45 Km South-West of Lusaka City in the Lusaka Province of Zambia. The Organization was founded in 2009 but officially registered with the Registrar of Societies in 2012.

Who We Are

Committed to Empowering the Lives of the Disadvantaged

Children have so much to offer to the world - we believe in the power that they hold, and know that they will mold future generations to come. Program Areas

Inclusive education for all

Since 2012, Twatasha Disabled and OVC's Organisation has been nurturing and supporting our community’s OVC's and persons with disabilities. All of our programs are designed with their best interests in mind. At Twatasha Disabled and OVC's Organisation, we provide our youth with the skills they need to thrive in society. From our Disability Workshops to our Fund Raising Opportunities - we have a variety of programs that are available to all. Browse through our services, and see the extent of what we have to offer.

Health, Education and HIV/AIDS Management

Making a Difference One Child at a Time

At Twatasha Disabled and OVC's Organisation, we strive to provide excellent home based care to persons with disabilities, OVC's and HIV/AIDS affected persons and infected persons irrespective of gender, religion or socio-economic background in the Kafue community through our variety of programs. Moreover, we aim to provide cheaper, affordable and accesible education to the OVC's and children with disabilities through the construction of a Boarding Special Unit Community Primary School, the first one ever in Kafue. We address key issues like early marriages and reproductive health more especially with girls and women being the primary target. HIV counselling and mitigation strategies are also part of our program

Governance, Leadership and Advocacy for human rights

Shaping the Leaders of Tomorrow

Since 2012, we have contributed to the development of children in the greater Kafue area through our wide range of programs. Our programs are centered on instilling strong values into our youth and provides a space for them to expand their mind while meeting new and interesting individuals. This includes

  • Engagement of both males and females in leadership trainings
  • Training and promotion of human rights
  • Engagement of traditional and civil leaders
  • Training of Neighbourhood Health Committee in community health planning
